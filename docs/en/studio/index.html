We combine the latest in drone, laser, and photogrammetric technology with the power of super computers to produce the highest quality images and 3D models. Iconem has developed an innovative processing method exclusively for recording threatened cultural heritage sites. The result of our partnerships with INRIA, France’s national computer science research centre, and Microsoft Research, our technology seamlessly integrates multiple levels of data to build extremely precise 3D models of cultural heritage sites. Our smart merging functionality flawlessly combines laser scanner, photogrammetry, and Lidar data in one model, ensuring the highest quality data and images. Infinite data processing integrates details, accurate to the millimetre, into site-wide scans, enabling new applications for big data in the cultural heritage field. Our technology uses faster processing speeds and reduces costs while producing a more photorealistic 3D model. Our data meet our high technical standards for accuracy and consistency to ensure that our realistic 3D models are optimised for archaeological assessment, architectural analysis, and all media forms.




Threatened at all times by looting, urbanization, mass tourism, armed conflicts and climate change, world heritage is today in danger. It is urgent to identify it. Founded in 2013, Iconem is a startup specializing in the 3D digitization of endangered sites. Its 3D models of monuments, urban or rural heritage on a large scale and its training courses dedicated to professionals aim at essential work of heritage conservation and restoration. For the sake of raising awareness, Iconem also creates digital exhibitions in France and internationally for the general public. These experiences of virtual reality and projection of 3D models allow visitors an immersive journey in exceptional places, often difficult to access.

About

Iconem


Founded in 2013, Iconem is an innovative startup that specialises in the digitisation of endangered cultural heritage sites in 3D. We work with international organisations, national governments, local authorities, and world class museums such as UNESCO, the Aga Khan Trust for Culture, the Sultanate of Oman, the City of Paris, and the Louvre. We design site-specific architectural 3D models; large-scale urban and rural 3D models; museum exhibitions; and training for local professionals.

Yves Ubelmann


President & Founder


Yves Ubelmann est architecte de formation, et a reçu son diplôme de l’école d’architecture de Versailles en 2006. De 2006 à 2010, il a travaillé comme architecte indépendant en Syrie, Iran, Afghanistan, au Pakistan, pour le relevé, l’étude et l’interprétation de sites archéologiques. Dans ce cadre, il a développé depuis 2009 une nouvelle approche de la photogrammétrie pour le relevé des sites archéologiques. De 2010 à 2011, il a travaillé comme ingénieur spécialisé au joint center Microsoft Research – INRIA, avec le département d’informatique de l’ENS (Ecole Normale Supérieure), pour développer et utiliser des méthodes de numérisations 3D basées sur des algorithmes de reconstruction photogrammétrique récents. En 2013, il a cofondé la société ICONEM, qui développe de nouvelles solutions de numérisation 3D d’environnements ou d’architectures, et travaille entre autres pour l’UNESCO ou la Banque Mondiale sur des projets internationaux de numérisation du patrimoine archéologique.

Alep - Voyage au coeur de 5.000 ans d’histoire

Villa Empain, Fondation Boghossian, Bruxelles, Belgium
Aleppo, located in the North-West of Syria, is known for its many emblematic architectural elements, classified as a World Heritage of Humanity by Unesco since 1986. Since 2012, Syria has faced numerous armed conflicts which have considerably damaged the old city of Aleppo. In order to preserve his memory and raise public awareness, the Boghossian Foundation is hosting an immersive and exclusively digital temporary exhibition presenting Iconem’s 3D documentation work, produced during several missions in 2017. This exhibition offers an immersive and memory experience through spectacular projections of 3D models produced by Iconem. It was possible to discover seven monuments of the old city of Aleppo : the Citadel and its neo-Hittite temple, the Umayyad Mosque, the Souk, the Ottoman house Beit Achiqbash, the Madrasa Al-Halawiyah as well as the Yalbougha hammam. Thanks to an augmented reality device, visitors are confronted in turn with the reality of the damage inflicted on the historic heart of the city and the beauty of the intact portions of these monuments.

Build an Empire : A virtual exhibition of the Roman worlds

Museum of Romanity, Nîmes, France
December 20, 2019 - March 8, 2020
The Iconem-produced exhibition “Build an empire” takes the visitor in the heart of seven archaeological sites of the ancient Roman Empire: Pompeii (Italy), Aphrodisias (Turkey), Garni (Armenia), Palmyra (Syria), Lepcis Magna (Libya), Carthago (Tunisia), Baelo Claudia (Spain). Thanks to the vast immersive projections of Iconem’s 3D models, this 100% digital exhibition is an invitation to discover the urban and architectural models of roman cities around the Mediterranean Sea. The exhibition focuses on the architectural and urban standards shared by the entire roman world, but also on their adaptation to the local culture.The triptych of projection off ers a striking and collective immersion into these roman sites. The exhibition ends with a virtual reality experience “Old Age Cities”, produced by Ubisoft and based on the Iconem data, which transport the visitor to the sites of Palmyra and Lepcis Magna.
Exhibition coordination: Xavier Gerard, Iconem project manager Artistic direction: Gaël Labousse, Artistic director Iconem Scientific advisor: Vivien Barrière, Lecturer in history and archeology at CY Cergy Paris University General scenography: Julien Baron

The fortifications of the City of Carcassonne were built in the Gallo-Roman era and  have experienced many upheavals over the centuries. Visitors can discover, noticing materials and architecture, the eventful episodes of the history of the City. On the other hand, it is more difficult to understand the importance of the ramparts in a broader context. Iconem was asked to propose a new visual language for representing heritage using the technique of aerial photogrammetry, by digitizing the entire City. The “Ellipse” project offers an artistic installation containing a 360° immersive space. This mediation place aims to promote local digital creation and to host an attractive program within its walls, even encouraging people from Carcassonne to re-visit this place in a new way.

Age-Old Cities

Arab World Institute, Paris, France
October 10, 2018 - February 17, 2019
This exhibition is organized in collaboration with Iconem, who produced all the 3D models recreating the four archaeological sites presented in the exhibition: Aleppo, Mosul, Palmyra, Leptis Magna, the ‘‘Age-Old Cities’’. The exhibition is truly immersive, in that it doesn’t showcase objects, but shows projections which alternatively display virtual reconstructions and 3D images of the damaged cities. The purpose of the exhibition is to raise public awareness on the destruction of universal heritage in times of conflict, while allowing visitors to explore these archaeological sites that are difficult to access. The exhibition also highlights the work of local communities to preserve the sites through several testimonies. Ubisoft, French video game studio, used Iconem’s data to curate a virtual reality experience which immerses visitors inside these sites.

Hippolyte, Paul, Auguste : Les Flandrin, Artistes et frères

Musée des Beaux Arts de Lyon, Lyon, France
The Saint-Germain-des-Près church, a former Benedictine abbey in Paris founded in the middle of the 6th century and restored in the 19th century by the architect Victor Baltard,  has always aroused the interest of its visitors. After a meticulous restoration of the church completed in 2020, Iconem’s team was commissioned by the Musée des Beaux-Arts de Lyon to digitize the interior of this monument featuring one of the most beautiful masterpieces produced by the painter Hippolyte Flandrin, assisted by his brother Paul. The exhibition was about its interior, and a part of it benefited from the research and technical analysis carried out by Iconem in support of the restoration project at Saint-Germain-des-Prés. An immersive projection was proposed, based on the digitization of all the interior decorations of the church carried out by our teams, in order to present them to visitors to help them understand the preparatory studies that were presented.

The Crac Des Chevaliers: Chronicles of a stone dream

Cité de l’architecture & du patrimoine, Paris, France
September 14, 2018 - March 4, 2019
‘‘Crac des Chevaliers’’, an exhibition co-produced by the Médiathèque de l’architecture et du patrimoine and the Cité de l’architecture et du patrimoine in collaboration with Iconem, explores this iconic Syrian monument through the prism of architecture and politics, by illustrating its symbolic place in Western imaginary. Iconem had already scanned a replica of the Crac des Chevaliers. For the exhibition, we created a 3D model of the building (accomplished on-site thanks to the support of UNESCO) and produced two videos linked to the data collection process. The first video explains this process, as well as the 3D digitization protocol. The second video offers an immersive journey through the Crac. These audiovisual productions allow visitors to explore the Crac des Chevaliers and discover some of its hidden features, like the castle’s interiors.

The Mont Saint-Michel, “Regards numérique sur la maquette”

Museum of Plans-Reliefs, Paris, France
October 11, 2018 - January 14, 2019
In 2018, the Paris-based Museum of Plans-Reliefs exhibited a mixed-reality experience for the first time. The “Mont St-Michel, regards numériques sur la maquette” exhibition enabled a renewed and innovative exploration of the scale model representing the Abbey and the city. Thanks to an exceptional partnership with Microsoft, supported by Iconem and Holoforge Interactive, the Museum offered a digital discovery of the Mont Saint-Michel and its scale model through a Hololens helmet (an independent holographic computer) to its visitors. Nineveh, Capital of a World Empire

National Museum of Antiquities, Leyden, Pays-Bas
October 20, 2017 - March 25, 2018
In 2017, Iconem was in Iraq to digitize remains of Nineveh found in tunnels used by ISIS, notably two lamassi, winged-bulls carved in stone. We produced a film featuring the tunnels and lamassis’ 3D models, which was screened at an exhibition at the Dutch National Museum of Antiquities, held in 2018. The exhibition featured more than 250 objects from Nineveh, one of the world’s most powerful cities 2,700 years ago. The Assyrian capital, located in northern Iraq, was a major cultural, military and economic hub. Sponsored by the UNESCO, the exhibition showed digital reconstructions of the (now lost) old city and the royal palace, created by an international academic team.
Curator: Lucas Petit and Daniele Morandi Bonacossi

Pyramides - Un voyage immersif au coeur des pyramides d’Egypte

Flyview, Paris, France
In 2018, Iconem’s team traveled to Egypt to carry out surveys of several groups of pyramids, using terrestrial recordings and drones. These recordings made it possible to digitize the pyramids in 3D and to create an immersive journey to the heart of the pyramids of Egypt. Produced in collaboration with the cultural leisure site Fly View Paris, this 360° virtual reality tour offers the public the opportunity to discover extraordinary places from a new perspective. The visit is carried out using virtual reality glasses and lasts approximately 18 minutes. Visitors were immersed in the heart of three major UNESCO World Heritage sites, and discovered Saqqara, Meidum and the Giza Plateau. By bringing these monuments to life through the magic of digital technology, Iconem and FlyView intend to raise awareness among the widest public of the crucial issues of heritage preservation.

I am Ashurbanipal : King Of the World, King of Assyria

The British Museum, London, United Kingdom
November 8, 2018 - February 24, 2019
Exhibited at the British Museum, ‘‘I am Ashurbanipal: King of the World, King of Assyria’’ is a journey into the world of ancient Assyria through the life and legacy of its last great ruler, King Ashurbanipal. The exhibition explores Ashurbanipal’s world through spectacular displays that evoke his palace and highlights the importance of preserving Iraq’s rich cultural heritage for future generations. Iconem contributed to the exhibition by producing a video on Nineveh, based on data collected on-site. The produced video includes before and after effects and positions selected archives on current maps, adding some historical perspective to our geographical understanding of the region. These effects allow visitors to grasp how the ancient Assyrian city overlaps with contemporary Mosul and acts as a valuable complement to the objects showcased in the rest of ‘‘I am Ashurbanipal: King of the World, King of Assyria’’.
General curator: Gareth Brereton, archaeologist and curator in charge of the Mesopotamian collection of the British Museum

Eternal Sites : From Bâmiyân to Palmyra

The Grand Palais, Paris, France
December 14, 2016 - January 9, 2017
The touring exhibition ‘‘Eternal Sites’’, produced by the Réunion des Musées Nationaux - Grand Palais and the Louvre Museum, in collaboration with Iconem, offers an immersion in the heart of four major endangered archaeological sites: the former capital of King Sargon in Khorsabad in Iraq, the Palmyra site, the Crac des Chevaliers and the Umayyad Great Mosque in Damascus, Syria. The mainly digital character of the exhibition facilitates its adaptation to new places. This 360° experience transports visitors to historic sites to raise awareness of heritage threatened or destroyed by armed conflict. The exhibition at the Grand Palais is organized into two sections, the Universal Site and the Images Laboratory. In the Universal Site, four films are screened, revealing on four major archeological sites, in a wide 360° panorama, allowing for an unprecedented immersive experience of the visitor.

Eternal Sites : From Bâmiyân to Palmyra

Bouregreg Cultural Complex, Sale, Morocco
October 30 - December 14, 2018
The touring exhibition ‘‘Eternal Sites’’, produced by the Reunion des Musées Nationaux – Grand Palais and the Louvre Museum, in collaboration with Iconem, offers an immersion in the heart of four major archaeological sites endangered: the former capital of King Sargon in Khorsabad in Iraq, the Palmyra site, the Crac des Chevaliers and the Umayyad Great Mosque in Damascus, Syria. Syrian Symphony : New Compositions in Sight And Sound

Aga Khan Museum, Toronto, Canada
May 20, 2017 - August 13, 2017
The exhibition ‘‘Syrian Symphony: new compositions in sight and sound” aims at exploring the roles of art and artists during conflicts, especially in Syria, through interactive and immersive works. ‘‘Syrian symphony: new compositions in sight and sound” is presented in association with Silkroad, the artistic organization created by reknown cellist Yo-Yo Ma. Similar to an orchestral symphony, the exhibition is divided into four distinct but connected ‘‘movements’’, including an immersive virtual creation entitled ‘‘Third Movement’’ and produced by Iconem. This experience presents monuments of the ancient Syrian city of Palmyra. The Temples of Bel and Baalshamin are presented, using the data collected during the intervention of Iconem on site, just days after the departure of ISIS. The video is synchronized to a soundscape composed and performed by the Silk Road Ensemble.
General curator: Amirali Alibhai, director of performing arts at the Aga Khan Museum, Scenography: Origin Studio, Sound production: Silk Road Ensemble
